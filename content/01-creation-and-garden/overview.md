# The Beginning of All Things

In the beginning, God created. That is where the story starts — not with a problem to be solved, but with a God who creates out of abundance, not necessity.

## The Pattern

Genesis 1–2 establishes a pattern the rest of Scripture strains toward: God present with His people, in a place He has made for them, in a state of wholeness and peace. Eden is not merely a location. It is a theological statement.

## What This Means for the Whole Story

Every act of redemption in Scripture is, in some sense, a return to what was lost here. The Tabernacle, the Temple, the Incarnation, the Church, the New Creation — all of them are God moving back toward His people. The story begins in a garden and ends in a garden-city precisely because God does not abandon His original design.
